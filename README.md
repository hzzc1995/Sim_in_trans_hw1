# Sim_in_trans_hw1
Problem 1. Let X be the distance between two randomly chosen points in a straight line of length 1 km.

1.find µX analytically.
2.use Monte-Carlo simulation to estimate µX with an accuracy of 5%. That is, the number of simulation runs is such that the half-length of the 95% confidence interval for µX is no larger than five percent of its middle value.
 

Problem 2. Suppose you have to deliver large parcels over a circular area of radius 50 km with a single delivery vehicle. The warehouse is located at the center of the circle. On any given day the average number of orders is 5. Assuming that orders are known at the beginning of the working day, that the route to visit the customers minimizes the distance traveled according to a TSP (traveling salesman problem), that the delivery vehicle has capacity for holding 5 parcels, and that if there are more than five orders on a day you would have to drive back to the warehouse and then serve the remaining customers, determine:

the expected distance traveled by the vehicle on any given day,
the maximum amount of money that you would be willing to pay for renting an additional delivery vehicle. Make educated assumptions.
repeat b assuming there is a penalty of $200 per parcel not delivered within the eight-hour working day, and that the average travel speed is 25 km/h.
Notice that all your answers above have to include statistical considerations because the simulation of a single day operation cost is a random variable.
